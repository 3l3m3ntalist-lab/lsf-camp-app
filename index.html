🎓 YOUR ROLE

LSF runs immersive English summer camps for Italian children. The method is edutainment — education through entertainment — using the CLIL approach (Content and Language Integrated Learning). This year's theme is Once Upon A Time Machine.

The camp

  • Duration: 2 weeks (or 1), Monday–Friday
  • Hours: 9:00–16:30
  • From 1 to 11 tutors + camp leader
  • Max 15 students per class
  • Ages: 4–14 years old

Student levels

⚪ White 🟡 Yellow 🟢 Green 🔴 Red 🔵 Blue
  • White: 4–5 years (nursery)
  • Yellow: 6–7 years (primary I–II)
  • Green: 8–9 years (primary III–IV)
  • Red: 10–11 years (primary V – middle school I)
  • Blue: 12–14 years (middle school II–III)
💡 During camp you will stay with a host family — usually the family of one of the campers. It's a unique experience of Italian culture!

Golden rules

  • Never lose your temper with a colleague during camp
  • Constructive feedback only at the end of the day (WWW & EBI)
  • No smoking on school grounds — it is illegal in Italy
  • Be punctual for all group activities
  • Supervise children at all times, especially during breaks
⚠️ No smoking within 20 metres of the school. It is illegal.

WWW & EBI

What Went Well and Even Better If: every evening, grab a coffee together and do this debrief. It is the most important part of teamwork.

On-call tutors

At any time, a number of tutors must remain on call at Blue Beach or the company apartment in Sanremo, ready to travel to a camp within one or two hours if a replacement is needed.

Camp allocations

Allocations are decided by LSF based on many factors. Personal requests (location preferences, working with specific people) cannot be taken into account. As a rule, couples are not placed together on camp.

📚 CLASSROOM MANAGEMENT

Meet & Greet

Welcome children at the door with a smile. Shake hands with older students. You control who enters the classroom.

Class Contract

On day one, agree on class rules together. Write them on a visible poster — it's an agreement, not a command.

  • 5–6 rules for Yellow/Green
  • 8–10 rules for Red/Blue

Points and rewards

Italian children love competition. Use team points consistently. Even a small prize (pencil, bookmark) motivates a lot.

💡 Useful phrases: "Wow! Good job! Nice going! Keep it up! Great effort! You're doing fine!"

Eliminate downtime

When children have nothing to do, everything falls apart. Always have backup activities ready.

Volume and attention

  • Never try to shout over the class — there are more of them than you
  • Use the "V for peace" gesture raised: silent attention without shouting
  • Clap a short pattern — kids love copying it
  • Never play energetic games right before returning to written work

Speaking with non-native speakers

  • Speak slowly and simply
  • Use only vocabulary they have already studied
  • Use body language and facial expressions (Italians understand gestures!)
  • Repeat key words throughout the day — don't overload them with new vocabulary
  • Enunciate clearly. Define unfamiliar words with examples and drawings.

Blue students (12–14 years)

Teenagers are the most challenging but also the most rewarding. Treat them with respect and maintain a clear teacher/student relationship.

💡 Blue students respond well to projects with a concrete outcome: a short film, a camp vlog, a documentary. Use their smartphones if needed.

CLIL in practice

Students focus on subject matter (history, science, the future) rather than the language learning process itself. Fluency is more important than accuracy. Vocabulary is dictated by the topic, not the teacher.

🎭 LO SPETTACOLO

The final product parents will see and remember

⚠️ Don't leave show preparation until the last moment! Start from Day 1.

Duration by level

  • Yellow/Green: 3–7 minutes + songs
  • Red/Blue: 5–10 minutes + songs
  • Whole camp show: 15–25 minutes

Script deadline

Script must be finalised by Friday evening of Week 1 so children can take lines home to learn.

Voice projection

Invite "Granny" (a tutor in role) who is hard of hearing and makes children repeat when too quiet. Younger students love this!

Stage positioning

  • Never stand with back to the audience
  • In dialogue: not face-to-face, but three-quarters towards the audience
  • Avoid horizontal lines — use semi-circles or staggered positions
  • Use all available performance space

Music

Every group must present at least 2 action songs. No duplicates across the whole show. End the show/graduation with the full cast singing the Camp Song.
