🎓 YOUR ROLE

LSF runs immersive English summer camps for Italian children. The method is edutainment — education through entertainment — using the CLIL approach (Content and Language Integrated Learning). This year's theme is Once Upon A Time Machine.

The camp

  • Duration: 2 weeks (or 1), Monday–Friday
  • Hours: 9:00–16:30
  • From 1 to 11 tutors + camp leader
  • Max 15 students per class
  • Ages: 4–14 years old

Student levels

⚪ White 🟡 Yellow 🟢 Green 🔴 Red 🔵 Blue
  • White: 4–5 years (nursery)
  • Yellow: 6–7 years (primary I–II)
  • Green: 8–9 years (primary III–IV)
  • Red: 10–11 years (primary V – middle school I)
  • Blue: 12–14 years (middle school II–III)
💡 During camp you will stay with a host family — usually the family of one of the campers. It's a unique experience of Italian culture!

Golden rules

  • Never lose your temper with a colleague during camp
  • Constructive feedback only at end of day (WWW & EBI)
  • No smoking on school grounds — it is illegal in Italy
  • Be punctual for all group activities
  • Supervise children at all times, especially during breaks
⚠️ No smoking within 20 metres of the school. It is illegal.

WWW & EBI

What Went Well and Even Better If: every evening, grab a coffee together and debrief as a team.

📚 CLASSROOM MANAGEMENT

Meet & Greet

Welcome children at the door with a smile. Shake hands with older students. You control who enters the classroom.

Class Contract

On day one, agree on class rules together. Write them on a visible poster — it's an agreement, not a command.

  • 5–6 rules for Yellow/Green
  • 8–10 rules for Red/Blue

Points and rewards

Italian children love competition. Use team points consistently.

💡 Useful phrases: "Wow! Good job! Nice going! Keep it up! Great effort! You're doing fine!"

Eliminate downtime

When children have nothing to do, everything falls apart. Always have backup activities ready.

Volume and attention

  • Never try to shout over the class
  • Use the raised "V for peace" gesture for silent attention
  • Clap a short pattern — kids love copying it
  • Never play energetic games right before written work

Speaking with non-native speakers

  • Speak slowly and simply
  • Use body language and facial expressions
  • Repeat key words throughout the day
  • Enunciate clearly and define unfamiliar words with examples

Blue students (12–14 years)

Treat them with respect and maintain a clear teacher/student relationship.

💡 Blue students respond well to projects with a concrete outcome: a short film, a camp vlog, a documentary.

🎭 LO SPETTACOLO

The final product parents will see and remember

⚠️ Don't leave show preparation until the last moment! Start from Day 1.

Duration by level

  • Yellow/Green: 3–7 minutes + songs
  • Red/Blue: 5–10 minutes + songs
  • Whole camp show: 15–25 minutes

Script deadline

Script must be finalised by Friday evening of Week 1 so children can take lines home to learn.

Stage positioning

  • Never stand with back to the audience
  • In dialogue: three-quarters towards the audience, not face-to-face
  • Avoid horizontal lines — use semi-circles or staggered positions

Music

Every group must present at least 2 action songs. No duplicates across the whole show. End with the full cast singing the Camp Song.
